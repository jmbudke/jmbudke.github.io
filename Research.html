Maternal Effects & Structure-Function Relationships       

  1. -Many maternal organisms protect their young offspring from threats and dangers. My research studies the structural abilities of maternal plants to protect their offspring from dehydration stress. This protection is provided by a thick waxy cuticle. The maternal cuticle forms early during development when the offspring is unable to protect itself. Functional tests revealed that the maternal cuticle is critical for offspring survival, development, and fitness.

  2. -We are currently analyzing cuticle chemistry to determine whether maternal cuticles are unique and potentially chemically specialized for protection.

Systematics & Taxonomy

  1. -Refining our systematic and taxonomic concepts is key to cataloging the planet’s biodiversity and broadening our understanding of plant evolution. Our concepts of species and the relationships between them are often initially based on morphology. Analyses using molecular phylogenetics and cytological studies can reveal novel relationships and uncover hidden diversity. In organisms thought to have few morphological features, such as mosses, homoplasy is regularly uncovered due to morphological reduction. Using the process of reciprocal illumination species can be further explored to determine whether our systematic and taxonomic concepts need to be revised. This may result in the description of new species (Isoëtes tennesseensis Luebke & Budke) or families (Micromitriaceae Smyth ex Goffinet & Budke).
